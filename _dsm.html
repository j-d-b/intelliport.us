DSM

Dynamic Schedule Module

Optimized human resource allocation based on demand

Background

Container terminal operations are inherently unpredictable; container ships do not arrive on a fixed schedule and schedules change with little notice. This unpredictability can make it difficult to properly and efficiently schedule human resources.

Additionally, due to differences in crane speeds, uneven distribution of work across quay and yard locations, and varying levels of congestion at different times of the day and week, productivity-based incentives are inequitable unless staff are evenly rotated across equipment and time-slots.

Thus, a dynamic scheduling system is needed to reduce idle staff and maximize productivity. This system must be able to revise the schedule at any time, adapting the staffing level to the changing demand.

Purpose

The Dynamic Schedule Module (DSM) automates and optimizes the creation of staff schedules to minimize the cost of human resources. Schedules are based on forecasted demand and can be manipulated dynamically when demand changes.

DSM determines hiring needs, assigns on-call employees to avoid overstaffing, and ensures equity in productivity-based incentives through equipment and shift rotation.

DSM also conforms to labor laws and company regulations.

Functionality

DSM offers two unique utilities:

  • A simulator, which helps recruitment planners determine hiring needs by generating the number of required new employees using a rolling-basis demand over a selected time interval (e.g. weeks, months, years).
  • A schedule generator, which builds cost-efficient schedules based on forecasted demand. It remains flexible by allowing schedules to be updated as demand changes.

Simulator

The simulator is a planning tool that determines the total number of employees that should be on staff. It uses expected demand to minimize the total required staff.

The simulator was tested in two container terminals (KAP and BCTC) and demonstrated a significant decrease in the required workforce for both.

Schedule Generator

The schedule generator creates cost-optimized schedules using demand, configuration of the labor force, labor laws and company regulations, and other cost factors. The list of inputs is customizable to conform to specific terminal needs.

The system calculates the absolute fixed cost that any employee must be paid when they are assigned to work. This incorporates any number of relevant costs, such as:

  • Salary
  • Overhead
  • Transportation
  • Food allowance

The absolute fixed cost is used to determine when it is beneficial to bring someone new in to work rather than having an on-site employee work overtime hours.

An on-call system is also employed to allow for unexpected demand increases to be met without paying the full rate of an on-site employee. On-call employees do not attend work, but may be called in at any time, at which point their rate will be increased to that of an on-site employee.

The concept of on-call staff is integral; implementing on-call staff prevents overstaffing and ensures all on-site workers are being utilized.

Thus, for a given time-block, staff can have one of the follow status:

  • Not scheduled
  • On-call
  • Scheduled to work on-site

Overtime and on-call can be assigned on a daily, weekly, or monthly basis.

On-Call

There is an average number of employees needed each day, but this number fluctuates from day to day. This is the basis for the on-call system.

An example:

  • A weekly plan estimates that ten employees will be needed on Tuesday and seven employees will be needed on Wednesday.
  • On Monday, the port is notified that a ship originally scheduled to arrive on Tuesday will not arrive until Wednesday.
  • Without an on-call system, three employees who are paid to be on-site on Tuesday, but will be idle. The next day, the seven scheduled employees will be unable to keep up with demand.
  • With an on-call system, on both days seven employees will be scheduled to be on-site and three employees will be scheduled for on-call. On Tuesday, the on-call employees will not be asked to attend work and there will be no idle employees. On Wednesday, the on-call employees will be asked to attend work and thus will be able to keep up with demand.

Thus, DSM minimizes cost by maximizing on-site staff utilization.

Scheduling Constraints

DSM considers the following:

  • Skill Level: The system allows the entry of a “skill level” (0-10 scale) based on productivity and ability. This is used to ensure that each shift has staff with a mix of skill levels, which facilitates consistent performance across all shifts.
  • Adjustments: The system allows immediate adjustments such as adding/dropping.
  • Shift Time: The system allows for the selection of varying start/end shift times for different employees and provides flexible shift lengths for all employees.
  • Equipment: The system allows multi-skilled staff members to rotate across equipment types.
  • Relief Staff: The system allows for allocation of relief staff.
  • Equity: The system ensures that all staff have equal productivity potential by evenly distributing:
    • Working hours
    • Overtime hours
    • Shift duration
    • Days off
    • Night and day shifts
    • Equipment assignment

Equity

Since some shifts are more productive than others, DSM rotates employees evenly between day and night shifts and balances days off between weekends and weekdays.

Additionally, cranes can have different operating speeds and frequency of use, so DSM rotates employees across equipment.

DSM’s unique rotation system provides equal opportunity for productivity to all employees. This allows terminals to fairly offer productivity-based incentives, promoting a positive and enthusiastic workforce.

The base system handles seniority and highly-skilled workers with higher pay; they are still rotated evenly with all other employees. However, the option exists to allow them to also have priority for certain preferred equipment or time-slots. This will, however, compromise the system’s equity measures and thus the ability to fairly offer productivity-based incentives.

Inputs

DSM accepts inputs related to:

Labor laws and company regulations
  • Daily working hours
  • Daily rest hours
  • Consecutive working hours
  • Consecutive working days
  • Weekly working hours
  • Weekly rest hours
  • Monthly working hours
  • Union regulations
Employees
  • Name
  • ID number
  • Position title
  • Qualifications
  • Skill level

Outputs

DSM produces the following output:

  • The total number of staff required over a selected time interval (e.g. weeks, months, years).
  • Cost-efficient schedules based on forecasted demand that can be updated at any time as demand changes.

System Integration

DSM can integrate with a Terminal Operating System (TOS) to automatically and dynamically receive the demand for work. It can also integrate with an Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP) system to output the exact hours each employee worked, including overtime and on-call hours, and therefore allow for the immediate generation of the payroll.
