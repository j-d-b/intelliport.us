DSM

Dynamic Schedule Module

Background

Container terminal operations are inherently unpredictable; container ships do not arrive on a fixed schedule and schedules can change within a day in advance. Additionally, due to differences in crane speeds, uneven distribution of work across yard locations, and varying levels of congestion at different times of the day and week, productivity-based incentives are inequitable unless staff are evenly rotated across equipment and time-slots.

Thus, a dynamic system is needed in order to reduce idle workers and maximize productivity. The system must be able to be revised at any time, adapting employee count to the changing demand.

Purpose

The Dynamic Schedule Module (DSM) automates and optimizes creation of staff schedules to minimize cost of human resources, satifying the above requirements. Schedules are based on forecasted demand and can be manipulated dynamically with demand changes.

DSM determines hiring needs, assigns on-call employees to avoid overstaffing, and enables productivity-based incentives through equitable position rotation.

DSM also conforms to given labor laws and company regulations, combining data with other cost factors to generate cost efficient schedules.

Functionality

DSM offers two unique utilities:

  • A simulator, which helps recruitment planners determine hiring needs by generating the number of required new employees using a rolling basis demand over a selected time interval (e.g. weeks, months, years).
  • A schedule generator, which builds cost-efficient schedules based on forecasted demand. It remains flexible by allowing schedules to be updated as demand changes.

Simulator

The simulator is a planning tool that determines the number of new employees to hire. It uses expected demand to minimize the number of required staff.

The concept of on-call staff is integral here; implementing on-call staff prevents overstaffing and ensures all on-site workers are being utilized.

The simulator mode has been trialed in two test terminals (KAP and BTCT) and demonstrated a striking 20% decrease in required workforce.

Schedule Generator

The schedule generator creates cost optimized schedules using demand, configuration of the labor force, labor law and company regulations, and other cost factors. The list of inputs is customizable to conform to specific terminal needs.

The system calculates the absolute fixed cost that any employee must be paid when they are assigned to work. This could incorporate any number of relevant costs, such as:

  • Salary
  • Overhead
  • Overtime
  • Transportation
  • Food allowance
  • Idling
  • On-call request

The absolute fixed cost is used to determine when it is beneficial to bring in someone new rather than having an existing worker continue into overtime hours.

An on-call system is also employed to allow for unexpected demand increases to be met without paying the full rate of an on-site employee. On-call employees do not attend work, but may be called in anytime, at which time their rate will raise to that of an on-site employee.

Thus, for a given time-block, staff can have one of the follow status:

  • Not scheduled
  • Scheduled to work on-site
  • On-call

Overtime and on-call can be assigned on a daily, weekly, or monthly basis.

On Call

There is an average number of employees typically needed a day, but there are fluctuations in the actual amount. The is the basis for the on-call system.

An example:

  • A weekly plan calls for 10 employees needed each day
  • A ship scheduled to arrive tomorrow reschedules for the day after
  • Without an on-call system, there will be staff who are paid to be on-site tomorrow, but are not needed. The next day, the on-site staff will be unable to keep up with demand.
  • The on-call system schedules a minimum number of staff to be on site and adpats to the fluctuations by keeping others available to come in.

Thus DSM minimizes cost by maximizing on-site staff utilization.

Scheduling Constraints

DSM considers the following:

  • Skill Level: The system allows the entry of a “skill level” (0-10 scale) based on productivity and ability. Staff with a mix of skill levels are assigned to each shift. This facilitates consistent performance across all shifts.
  • Add/Drop:The system allows immediate adjustments such as adding/dropping.
  • Shift Time: The system allows the selection of different start/end times of the shift of a specific employee and provides flexible shift lengths for all employees.
  • Equipment: The system allows a multi-skilled staff member to rotate across equipment types.
  • Relief Staff: The system allows for allocation of relief staff.
  • Equity: The system ensures that all staff have equal productivity potential by evenly distributing:
    • Working hours
    • Overtime hours
    • Time-slots
    • Days off
    • Night and day shifts
    • Equipment assignment

Equity

Since some shifts are more productive than others, DSM rotates employees evenly between day and night shifts, and balances days off between weekends and weekdays.

Additionally, cranes can have different operating speeds, and often certain cranes are used more frequently than others, due to location. Thus, DSM rotates employees across equipment as well.

DSM’s unique rotation system provides equal opportunity for productivity to all employees. This allows terminals to fairly offer productivity-based incentives, promoting a positive and enthusiastic workforce.

The base system handles seniority and highly skilled workers with higher pay; they are still rotated evenly with all other employees. However, the option exists in input to allow them to also have priority in which machine they operate or what time-slots they prefer. This will, however, compromise the system’s equity measures and thus the ability to fairly give productivity-based incentives.

Inputs

DSM accepts inputs related to:

Labor laws and company regulations
  • Daily working hours
  • Daily rest hours
  • Consecutive working hours
  • Consecutive working days
  • Weekly working hours
  • Weekly rest hours
  • Monthly working hours
  • Union regulations
Employees
  • Name
  • ID number
  • Position title
  • Qualifications
  • Skill level

System Integration

DSM can integrate with a Terminal Operating System (TOS) to automatically and dynamically receive the demand for work. It can also integrate with a human resource system to provide the exact working hours for each staff, including overtime and on-call hours, and therefore allow immediate generation of the payroll.
